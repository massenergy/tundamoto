Households

The Household Level

Income at the household level, guaranteed basic income, that is what unlocks dreams and allows a people to be adventurous and innovative.

Tundamoto project aims to facilitate the planting of 2,000 dragon fruit plants per each household, for One million households in Kenya's ASAL regions.

The project will take 10 to 20 years to reach those one million households and will place over one million acres under permanent plant cover.

Households in marginalized ASAL regions will be transformed both economically, politically and socially.

Lives

Smallholder Farmers

The current economic bedrock of Kenya rests on the Swynnerton Plan of the 1950's. This was an agricultural plan formulated by R.J. M. Swynnerton who was an agricultural officer and its goal was empowering small holder African farmers to be able to plant and own cash crops. The plan was funded with a grant of 5 million pounds from the British Government.

Tundamoto project, 70 years later, will have an order of magnitude more impact than the colonial government Swynnerton plan.

ASAL lands account for over 80% of the land mass of Kenya.
